Secondary Education Quality in Malaysia

Global Secondary Education Enrolment Ratio, 2021

Insights into Student Demographics and Completion Rate

Number of students (2016-2021)

Selangor has the highest number of students. This is due to its larger population size and greater development compared to other states. The number of students grew by 0.86% between 2016 and 2021.

On the other hand, Labuan and Putrajaya have relatively low student numbers, with maximum of 10,464 and 6,549 students respectively in 2021.

Completion rate in Upper Secondary

Completion rate may exceed 100% due to students repeating grades or transferring between states.

Despite having the largest student population, Selangor does not achieve the highest completion rates. This could be attributed to various economic factors, where families in urban regions may face pressures that result in students dropping out.

Putrajaya has a low student population and completion rate as it is primarily an administrative center with fewer schools.

Insights into Teacher Demographics and Gender

Number of teachers over two decades

All states experienced an increase in the number of teachers over the 20-year period.

Notably, Putrajaya had no teachers in 2001, as it was only established in 1995, resulting in the absence of schools at that time.

Besides, Selangor, having a larger student population, has the highest number of teachers compared to other states.

Gender distribution

The proportion of female teachers is greater than that of male teachers in every state.

It has been claimed that Malaysia has up to 40% more women in the workforce compared to other ASEAN countries.

This trend could be influenced by traits often stereotypically linked to women, such as empathy, patience, and caregiving.
